Veriba · Design Direction 03

Four ways to breathe

What the reference images actually run on: two colors, one subject, and emptiness doing the composition. A packed 2px grid is the opposite tone. Below, four feed structures that trade density for air — photos untouched (no masks, no scrims), captions set on the canvas beneath them like gallery labels.

A · Gallery hang
One column, alternating weight
Every case gets its own wall. Images alternate size and alignment — large left, small right — the way a curator hangs a room. Maximum air, slowest browse.

Shared rules across all four: photos are never masked, tinted, or captioned over — labels sit on the plaster beneath them like gallery cards · warm brown shadows lift each image off the canvas · the verified mark becomes a small teal seal in the caption line · filter chips collapse to one quiet small-caps line · copper appears only in italics and rules; teal only in the seal.

The trade: A shows ~1.5 cases per screen, B ~4, C exactly 1, D ~3. Instagram optimizes for 12 — that's the tone you're leaving behind on purpose.
